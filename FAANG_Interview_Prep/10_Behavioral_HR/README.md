# 10 — Behavioral & HR Questions (FAANG Interview Guide)

## 🎯 Why This Matters
FAANG companies reject technically strong candidates who fail behavioral rounds. Amazon has 14 Leadership Principles. Google evaluates "Googleyness." This round determines culture fit.

---

## 1. STAR Method (Use for EVERY answer)

```
S — SITUATION:  Set the context (project, team, timeline)
T — TASK:       What was YOUR specific responsibility?
A — ACTION:     What did YOU do? (use "I", not "we")
R — RESULT:     Quantifiable outcome (numbers, metrics, impact)
```

### Example

> **Q: "Tell me about a time you disagreed with a teammate."**

**S:** "During a backend project, our team debated whether to use MongoDB or PostgreSQL for an e-commerce platform."

**T:** "As the backend lead, I needed to make the final recommendation to the tech lead."

**A:** "I created a comparison document covering schema complexity, transaction requirements, and scaling needs. I set up a meeting where both sides presented their case. The data showed we needed ACID transactions for payments, so I recommended PostgreSQL with a Redis cache for product catalog reads."

**R:** "The team aligned on the decision. We shipped on time, and the payment system handled 50K transactions in the first month with zero data inconsistency issues."

## 2. Must-Prepare Questions

### "Tell me about yourself" (2 minutes max)

```
FORMULA: Present → Past → Future

"I'm a [role] with experience in [tech stack].
 Most recently, I [current project/achievement].
 Before that, I [relevant past experience].
 I'm excited about [company name] because [specific reason]."
```

**Example:**
"I'm a full-stack developer focused on Node.js and React. I recently built a real-time notification system handling 10K concurrent users using WebSockets and Redis. Before that, I interned at [company] where I optimized API response times by 40% through Redis caching. I'm excited about [Company] because your engineering blog on microservices migration really resonated with my interest in distributed systems."

---

### "Why this company?"

**Research these before the interview:**
- Recent product launches or features
- Engineering blog posts
- Company mission and values
- Tech stack they use
- Recent news or funding

**Template:**
"Three reasons: First, [specific product/feature] solves a real problem I care about. Second, your engineering team's work on [specific blog post/talk] aligns with my technical interests. Third, [company value] resonates with how I approach engineering."

---

### "What is your greatest weakness?"

```
FORMULA: Real weakness + What you're doing about it

❌ BAD: "I'm a perfectionist" / "I work too hard"
✅ GOOD: "I used to struggle with estimating task timelines — 
         I'd underestimate complexity. I've started breaking tasks 
         into smaller subtasks and adding 20% buffer. My estimates 
         have improved significantly over the last 6 months."
```

## 3. Common Behavioral Questions + Frameworks

### Conflict / Disagreement
> "Tell me about a time you disagreed with a teammate / manager."

**Key points:**
- Show you listen to the other side
- Focus on data/facts, not emotions
- Show compromise or escalation process
- End with positive outcome

---

### Failure
> "Tell me about a time you failed."

**Key points:**
- Pick a REAL failure (not a humble brag)
- Own it (don't blame others)
- Focus 70% on what you LEARNED
- Show how you applied the lesson later

**Example:**
"I deployed a database migration without a rollback script. It corrupted 200 user records. I immediately notified the team, we restored from backup within 2 hours. After that, I created a deployment checklist that became our team standard: migration script, rollback script, backup verification. We've had zero data incidents since."

---

### Leadership
> "Tell me about a time you took initiative / led without authority."

**Key points:**
- Show you identified a problem others ignored
- You took action without being asked
- You rallied people or built consensus
- Measurable positive outcome

---

### Time Pressure
> "Tell me about a time you had a tight deadline."

**Key points:**
- Show prioritization (what you cut vs kept)
- Communication with stakeholders
- How you managed stress
- Delivered on time (or managed expectations early)

## 4. Questions to Ask the Interviewer

### For Engineering Manager
- "What does a typical sprint cycle look like?"
- "How do you handle technical debt vs feature development?"
- "What's the biggest engineering challenge the team faces right now?"
- "How do you measure success for this role in the first 6 months?"

### For Engineers
- "What's your tech stack and how has it evolved?"
- "What does code review look like here?"
- "What's one thing you'd improve about the engineering culture?"
- "Can you walk me through a recent project you worked on?"

### Red Flag Questions (shows you care about growth)
- "What does the on-call rotation look like?"
- "How does the team handle production incidents?"
- "What learning/growth opportunities are available?"

## 5. Amazon Leadership Principles (Top 6 Most Asked)

| Principle | What They're Looking For | Prepare a Story About... |
|-----------|------------------------|--------------------------|
| **Customer Obsession** | Decisions driven by customer needs | Time you went above and beyond for a user |
| **Ownership** | Think long-term, never say "that's not my job" | Time you took responsibility beyond your role |
| **Bias for Action** | Take calculated risks, don't wait for perfect info | Time you made a quick decision with limited data |
| **Dive Deep** | Get into details, verify with data | Time you debugged a complex issue |
| **Earn Trust** | Listen, be honest, self-critical | Time you admitted a mistake or gave tough feedback |
| **Deliver Results** | Focus on outcomes despite obstacles | Time you shipped under pressure |

## 6. Salary Negotiation

```
RULES:
1. Never give a number first — "What's the budget for this role?"
2. Research market rate (levels.fyi, glassdoor, blind)
3. Negotiate AFTER offer (not during interview)
4. Negotiate total compensation (base + bonus + equity + benefits)
5. Always be polite and professional

SCRIPT:
"Thank you for the offer! I'm very excited about the role.
 Based on my research and experience, I was expecting something
 closer to [X]. Is there flexibility on the base salary?
 I'd also love to discuss the equity/signing bonus component."

If they can't move on salary:
"I understand budget constraints. Would you consider 
 [extra PTO / signing bonus / early review / remote flexibility]?"
```

  deadline, technical challenge, teamwork, initiative

□ Prepare 3-5 questions for the interviewer

□ Have water, pen, paper nearby
