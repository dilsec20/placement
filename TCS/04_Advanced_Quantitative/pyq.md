## 1. Advanced Algebra

**Q1.** If x + 1/x = 5, find the value of x² + 1/x².
- (a) 23  (b) 25  (c) 27  (d) 21

> **Answer: (a) 23**
> (x + 1/x)² = x² + 2 + 1/x² = 25 → x² + 1/x² = 23

---

**Q2.** If x + 1/x = 5, find x³ + 1/x³.
- (a) 100  (b) 110  (c) 115  (d) 120

> **Answer: (b) 110**
> x³ + 1/x³ = (x + 1/x)³ − 3(x + 1/x) = 125 − 15 = 110

---

**Q3.** If a + b + c = 0, find (a² + b² + c²) / (a² − bc).
- (a) 1  (b) 2  (c) 3  (d) 0

> **Answer: (b) 2**
> When a+b+c=0: a²+b²+c² = 2(b²+c²+bc) and a²−bc = b²+bc+c². Result = 2.

---

**Q4.** If a³ + b³ + c³ = 3abc and a + b + c ≠ 0, what can be concluded?
- (a) a = b = c
- (b) a + b + c = 0
- (c) Either a = b = c or a + b + c = 0
- (d) None of these

> **Answer: (c) Either a = b = c or a + b + c = 0**
> a³+b³+c³−3abc = (a+b+c)(a²+b²+c²−ab−bc−ca). This = 0 when either factor = 0.

---

**Q5.** If a − b = 3 and a² + b² = 29, find the value of ab.
- (a) 10  (b) 12  (c) 8  (d) 15

> **Answer: (a) 10**
> (a−b)² = a²−2ab+b² → 9 = 29−2ab → 2ab = 20 → ab = 10

## 2. Quadratic Equations

**Q6.** If one root of x² − 5x + k = 0 is 2, find k and the other root.
- (a) k=6, root=3  (b) k=4, root=3  (c) k=6, root=4  (d) k=8, root=3

> **Answer: (a) k=6, root=3**
> Substituting x=2: 4−10+k=0 → k=6. Sum of roots=5 → other root=3. Check: 2×3=6=k ✓

---

**Q7.** Find the nature of roots of 3x² − 4x + 5 = 0.
- (a) Real and distinct  (b) Real and equal  (c) Complex  (d) Rational

> **Answer: (c) Complex**
> D = 16 − 60 = −44 < 0 → No real roots (complex)

---

**Q8.** If α and β are roots of x² − 7x + 12 = 0, find α² + β².
- (a) 25  (b) 37  (c) 49  (d) 24

> **Answer: (a) 25**
> α+β = 7, αβ = 12. α²+β² = (α+β)² − 2αβ = 49 − 24 = 25

---

**Q9.** Form the quadratic equation whose roots are 3 and −5.
- (a) x²+2x−15=0  (b) x²−2x−15=0  (c) x²−2x+15=0  (d) x²+2x+15=0

> **Answer: (a) x²+2x−15=0**
> Sum = −2, Product = −15. Equation: x²−(−2)x+(−15) = x²+2x−15 = 0

## 3. Inequalities

**Q10.** Solve: x² − 7x + 10 ≤ 0
- (a) 2 ≤ x ≤ 5  (b) x ≤ 2 or x ≥ 5  (c) x ≤ 2  (d) x ≥ 5

> **Answer: (a) 2 ≤ x ≤ 5**
> (x−2)(x−5) ≤ 0. Since coefficient of x² is positive: solution between roots.

---

**Q11.** Solve: |2x − 3| < 7
- (a) −2 < x < 5  (b) −5 < x < 2  (c) x < 5  (d) x > −2

> **Answer: (a) −2 < x < 5**
> −7 < 2x−3 < 7 → −4 < 2x < 10 → −2 < x < 5

## 4. Functions & Graphs

**Q12.** If f(x) = 2x + 3 and g(x) = x², find f(g(2)).
- (a) 7  (b) 11  (c) 14  (d) 9

> **Answer: (b) 11**
> g(2) = 4. f(4) = 2(4) + 3 = 11

---

**Q13.** Find the minimum value of f(x) = x² + 4x + 7.
- (a) 7  (b) 3  (c) 1  (d) 4

> **Answer: (b) 3**
> f(x) = (x+2)² + 3. Minimum = 3 at x = −2.

---

**Q14.** If f(x) = 3x − 1, find f⁻¹(x).
- (a) (x+1)/3  (b) (x−1)/3  (c) 3x+1  (d) (3+x)/1

> **Answer: (a) (x+1)/3**
> y = 3x−1 → x = (y+1)/3. So f⁻¹(x) = (x+1)/3

## 5. Logarithms

**Q15.** Find log₂(64).
- (a) 4  (b) 5  (c) 6  (d) 8

> **Answer: (c) 6**
> 2⁶ = 64. So log₂(64) = 6

---

**Q16.** If log₁₀(2) = 0.301, find log₁₀(50).
- (a) 1.699  (b) 1.602  (c) 2.301  (d) 1.799

> **Answer: (a) 1.699**
> log₁₀(50) = log₁₀(100/2) = 2 − 0.301 = 1.699

---

**Q17.** Simplify: log₃(27) + log₃(9) − log₃(81)
- (a) 0  (b) 1  (c) 2  (d) 3

> **Answer: (b) 1**
> = log₃(3³) + log₃(3²) − log₃(3⁴) = 3 + 2 − 4 = 1

---

**Q18.** Solve: log₂(x) + log₂(x−2) = 3
- (a) x = 4  (b) x = 6  (c) x = 2  (d) x = 8

> **Answer: (a) x = 4**
> log₂[x(x−2)] = 3 → x²−2x = 8 → x²−2x−8 = 0 → (x−4)(x+2) = 0. x = 4 (reject −2).

## 6. Set Theory

**Q19.** In a class of 100 students, 60 like Cricket, 50 like Football, and 20 like both. How many like neither?
- (a) 5  (b) 10  (c) 15  (d) 20

> **Answer: (b) 10**
> n(C ∪ F) = 60 + 50 − 20 = 90. Neither = 100 − 90 = 10

---

**Q20.** In a survey of 200 people, 120 read newspaper A, 100 read B, 80 read C, 50 read A&B, 40 read B&C, 30 read A&C, and 10 read all three. How many read at least one?
- (a) 180  (b) 190  (c) 200  (d) 170

> **Answer: (b) 190**
> n(A∪B∪C) = 120+100+80−50−40−30+10 = 190

---

**Q21.** From Q20, how many read exactly one newspaper?
- (a) 90  (b) 100  (c) 80  (d) 110

> **Answer: (a) 90**
> Only A = 120−50−30+10 = 50. Only B = 100−50−40+10 = 20. Only C = 80−40−30+10 = 20.
> Total exactly one = 50+20+20 = 90

## 7. Advanced Permutation & Combination

**Q22.** In how many ways can 5 boys and 3 girls sit in a row such that no two girls are adjacent?
- (a) 14400  (b) 7200  (c) 28800  (d) 3600

> **Answer: (a) 14400**
> Arrange 5 boys: 5! = 120. Gaps: _B_B_B_B_B_ = 6 gaps.
> Choose 3 from 6: ⁶C₃ = 20. Arrange girls: 3! = 6.
> Total = 120 × 20 × 6 = 14,400

---

**Q23.** Find the number of derangements of 4 elements.
- (a) 6  (b) 9  (c) 12  (d) 3

> **Answer: (b) 9**
> D(4) = 4![1 − 1 + 1/2 − 1/6 + 1/24] = 24 × 3/8 = 9

---

**Q24.** How many 4-letter words can be formed from the word "MATHEMATICS"?
- (a) 2454  (b) 756  (c) 1680  (d) 2310

> **Answer: Depends on counting repeated letters**
> MATHEMATICS has: M(2), A(2), T(2), H(1), E(1), I(1), C(1), S(1) — 8 distinct, 3 repeated pairs.
> This requires case-by-case: all distinct, 2 same + 2 distinct, 2 same + 2 same.
> **Typically answer = (a) 2454** (in TCS pattern questions)

---

**Q25.** In how many ways can 7 people sit around a circular table if 2 specific people must NOT sit adjacent?
- (a) 480  (b) 720  (c) 240  (d) 360

> **Answer: (a) 480**
> Total circular = (7−1)! = 720. Adjacent = 2 × (6−1)! = 2 × 120 = 240.
> Not adjacent = 720 − 240 = 480

## 8. Advanced Probability

**Q26.** A bag has 5 red and 3 blue balls. Two balls are drawn without replacement. Probability both are red?
- (a) 5/14  (b) 25/64  (c) 5/28  (d) 10/28

> **Answer: (a) 5/14**
> P = 5/8 × 4/7 = 20/56 = 5/14

---

**Q27.** Three coins are tossed. Find P(at least 2 heads).
- (a) 3/8  (b) 1/2  (c) 5/8  (d) 1/4

> **Answer: (b) 1/2**
> P(2H) + P(3H) = 3/8 + 1/8 = 4/8 = 1/2

---

**Q28.** Two dice are rolled. What is P(sum ≥ 10)?
- (a) 1/6  (b) 5/36  (c) 1/12  (d) 7/36

> **Answer: (a) 1/6**
> Sum 10: 3 ways, Sum 11: 2 ways, Sum 12: 1 way = 6/36 = 1/6

---

**Q29.** A box has 4 defective and 6 good items. 3 are drawn randomly. P(at most one defective)?
- (a) 2/3  (b) 1/2  (c) 5/6  (d) 3/4

> **Answer: (a) 2/3**
> P(0 def) = ⁶C₃/¹⁰C₃ = 20/120 = 1/6.
> P(1 def) = (⁴C₁×⁶C₂)/¹⁰C₃ = 60/120 = 1/2.
> P(≤1) = 1/6 + 1/2 = 2/3

---

**Q30.** A bag contains 3 white and 5 black balls. If 4 balls are drawn, what is the probability of getting exactly 2 white balls?
- (a) 3/7  (b) 5/14  (c) 3/14  (d) 2/7

> **Answer: (a) 3/7**
> P = (³C₂ × ⁵C₂)/⁸C₄ = (3 × 10)/70 = 30/70 = 3/7

## 9. Geometry & Mensuration

**Q31.** A cylinder has radius 7 cm and height 10 cm. Find the volume.
- (a) 1540 cm³  (b) 1500 cm³  (c) 1440 cm³  (d) 1386 cm³

> **Answer: (a) 1540 cm³**
> V = πr²h = 22/7 × 49 × 10 = 1540 cm³

---

**Q32.** The diagonal of a cube is 6√3 cm. Find its volume.
- (a) 216 cm³  (b) 125 cm³  (c) 343 cm³  (d) 64 cm³

> **Answer: (a) 216 cm³**
> Diagonal = a√3 = 6√3 → a = 6. Volume = 6³ = 216

---

**Q33.** A sphere and a cube have the same surface area. Find the ratio of their volumes.
- (a) √(6/π) : 1  (b) √π : √6  (c) 6 : π  (d) √6 : √π

> **Answer: (d) √6 : √π**
> 4πr² = 6a². r/a = √(6/4π) = √(3/2π). 
> V_sphere/V_cube = (4πr³/3)/a³ = ... = √6 : √π

---

**Q34.** The area of an equilateral triangle with side 8 cm is:
- (a) 16√3 cm²  (b) 32 cm²  (c) 24√3 cm²  (d) 64√3 cm²

> **Answer: (a) 16√3 cm²**
> Area = (√3/4) × 8² = (√3/4) × 64 = 16√3

---

**Q35.** A container in the shape of a cone has radius 7 cm and height 24 cm. Find the slant height.
- (a) 25 cm  (b) 31 cm  (c) 24 cm  (d) 26 cm

> **Answer: (a) 25 cm**
> l = √(r²+h²) = √(49+576) = √625 = 25 cm (7-24-25 triplet)

**Q39.** Find the slope of the line passing through (1,2) and (4,8).

## 12. Number Theory (Advanced)

**Q43.** Find the number of factors of 360.
- (a) 20  (b) 24  (c) 18  (d) 36

> **Answer: (b) 24**
> 360 = 2³ × 3² × 5¹. Factors = (3+1)(2+1)(1+1) = 4×3×2 = 24

---

**Q44.** Highest power of 2 in 20!
- (a) 16  (b) 18  (c) 15  (d) 10

> **Answer: (b) 18**
> ⌊20/2⌋ + ⌊20/4⌋ + ⌊20/8⌋ + ⌊20/16⌋ = 10 + 5 + 2 + 1 = 18

---

**Q45.** What is the remainder when 3^100 is divided by 7?
- (a) 1  (b) 2  (c) 4  (d) 6

> **Answer: (c) 4**
> 3¹≡3, 3²≡2, 3³≡6, 3⁴≡4, 3⁵≡5, 3⁶≡1 (mod 7). Cycle=6.
> 100 mod 6 = 4. 3⁴ mod 7 = 81 mod 7 = 4

## 13. Progressions (AP, GP, HP)

**Q46.** Find the sum of first 20 terms of AP: 5, 8, 11, 14, ...
- (a) 670  (b) 680  (c) 690  (d) 660

> **Answer: (a) 670**
> a=5, d=3, n=20. S = n/2[2a+(n−1)d] = 10[10+57] = 10×67 = 670

---

**Q47.** Three numbers are in GP. Their sum is 21 and product is 216. Find the numbers.
- (a) 3, 6, 12  (b) 4, 6, 9  (c) 2, 6, 18  (d) 6, 6, 6

> **Answer: (a) 3, 6, 12**
> Product = a³ → a = 6 (middle term). Let terms be 6/r, 6, 6r.
> Sum: 6/r + 6 + 6r = 21 → 6/r + 6r = 15. r = 2 or 1/2.
> Numbers: 3, 6, 12 (or 12, 6, 3)

---

**Q48.** The 5th term of a GP is 48 and the 8th term is 384. Find the common ratio.
- (a) 2  (b) 3  (c) 4  (d) 1/2

> **Answer: (a) 2**
> ar⁷/ar⁴ = r³ = 384/48 = 8. r = 2

## 14. Data Interpretation

**Q49.** Study the following pie chart of monthly expenses (Total = ₹50,000):

| Category | Percentage |
|----------|-----------|
| Food | 30% |
| Rent | 25% |
| Transport | 15% |
| Education | 20% |
| Savings | 10% |

**(i)** How much is spent on Food?
> **Answer:** 30% of 50,000 = **₹15,000**

**(ii)** What is the ratio of Rent to Transport?
> **Answer:** 25:15 = **5:3**

**(iii)** If savings increase by 50%, what is the new savings amount?
> **Answer:** Original = 5000. New = 5000 × 1.5 = **₹7,500**

---

**Q50.** A company's revenue for 5 quarters:

| Quarter | Revenue (₹ Lakh) |
|---------|-----------------|
| Q1 | 120 |
| Q2 | 150 |
| Q3 | 180 |
| Q4 | 140 |
| Q5 | 200 |

**(i)** What is the percentage increase from Q1 to Q5?
> **Answer:** (200−120)/120 × 100 = **66.67%**

**(ii)** Which quarter had the highest growth rate over the previous quarter?
> **Answer:** Q1→Q2: 25%, Q2→Q3: 20%, Q3→Q4: −22.2%, Q4→Q5: 42.9%. **Q5** had highest growth (42.9%).

**(iii)** What is the average quarterly revenue?
> **Answer:** (120+150+180+140+200)/5 = 790/5 = **₹158 Lakh**
