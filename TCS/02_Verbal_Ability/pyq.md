## 1. Reading Comprehension

### Passage 1 (Technology Theme — Most Common in TCS)

> Artificial Intelligence has made significant strides in recent years, particularly in natural language processing and computer vision. However, experts warn that current AI systems lack true understanding and consciousness. They operate through pattern recognition on massive datasets rather than genuine comprehension. This distinction is crucial because it determines the limits of what AI can reliably accomplish. Tasks requiring common sense reasoning, emotional intelligence, or ethical judgment remain challenging for even the most advanced AI systems.

**Q1.** What is the main idea of the passage?
- (a) AI has become conscious
- (b) AI is powerful but has fundamental limitations
- (c) AI will never improve
- (d) Pattern recognition is useless

> **Answer: (b)** — The passage acknowledges AI progress but emphasizes its limitations.

**Q2.** The word "strides" in the passage most nearly means:
- (a) Steps while walking
- (b) Failures
- (c) Significant progress
- (d) Slow movements

> **Answer: (c)** — In context, "made significant strides" = significant progress.

**Q3.** According to the passage, current AI systems function through:
- (a) Emotional intelligence
- (b) Ethical judgment
- (c) Pattern recognition on massive datasets
- (d) True understanding

> **Answer: (c)** — Directly stated in the passage.

---

### Passage 2 (Environment Theme)

> Climate change is not just an environmental issue; it is a social and economic crisis. Rising sea levels threaten coastal communities, while changing rainfall patterns disrupt agriculture in developing nations. The World Bank estimates that by 2050, climate change could push an additional 132 million people into extreme poverty. Despite international agreements such as the Paris Accord, global carbon emissions continue to rise. Many scientists argue that without radical changes in energy policy and consumption patterns, the targets set by these agreements will remain unattainable.

**Q4.** According to the passage, climate change is:
- (a) Only an environmental issue
- (b) A social, economic, and environmental crisis
- (c) Easily solvable
- (d) Not a serious concern

> **Answer: (b)** — The opening sentence clearly states it's not just environmental.

**Q5.** The tone of the author is:
- (a) Optimistic
- (b) Indifferent
- (c) Concerned and cautionary
- (d) Humorous

> **Answer: (c)** — Words like "threaten," "extreme poverty," and "unattainable" indicate concern.

**Q6.** Which of the following can be inferred from the passage?
- (a) The Paris Accord has successfully reduced emissions
- (b) Current efforts may not be sufficient to meet climate goals
- (c) Developing nations are not affected
- (d) Sea levels are decreasing

> **Answer: (b)** — The passage states emissions "continue to rise" despite agreements.

## 2. Error Identification / Correction

**Q7.** Find the error: "Each of the boys **have completed** their homework on time."
- (a) Each of the boys
- (b) have completed
- (c) their homework
- (d) on time
- (e) No error

> **Answer: (b)** — "Each" takes singular verb → "has completed." Also "their" should be "his."

---

**Q8.** "Everyday life **have become** more complicated with the advancement in mobile technology."
- (a) Everyday life
- (b) have become
- (c) more complicated
- (d) with the advancement
- (e) No error

> **Answer: (b)** — "Life" is singular → "has become"

---

**Q9.** "She **is working** here **since 2020** and plans to continue."
- (a) She
- (b) is working
- (c) since 2020
- (d) and plans to continue
- (e) No error

> **Answer: (b)** — "Since" requires present perfect continuous → "has been working"

---

**Q10.** "Neither the teacher **nor** the students **was** present in the class yesterday."
- (a) Neither
- (b) nor
- (c) was
- (d) in the class yesterday
- (e) No error

> **Answer: (c)** — With "neither...nor," verb agrees with the nearest subject → "students" (plural) → "were"

---

**Q11.** "He is one of those people who **believes** in hard work."
- (a) He is
- (b) one of those people
- (c) who believes
- (d) in hard work
- (e) No error

> **Answer: (c)** — "who" refers to "people" (plural) → "who believe"

---

**Q12.** "The team of players **are** ready for the tournament."
- (a) The team
- (b) of players
- (c) are ready
- (d) for the tournament
- (e) No error

> **Answer: (c)** — "Team" is a singular collective noun → "is ready"

## 3. Fill in the Blanks

**Q13.** The manager was _______ by the employee's _______ attitude toward work.
- (a) pleased, diligent
- (b) annoyed, casual
- (c) surprised, regular
- (d) happy, negligent

> **Answer: (b)** — "annoyed" pairs logically with "casual" (negative pair)

---

**Q14.** Despite his _______ efforts, the project remained _______.
- (a) tireless, incomplete
- (b) minimal, successful
- (c) half-hearted, finished
- (d) significant, advanced

> **Answer: (a)** — "Despite" indicates contrast between effort and result.

---

**Q15.** The lady _______ purse was lost lodged a complaint at the police station.
- (a) who
- (b) whom
- (c) whose
- (d) which

> **Answer: (c)** — Possessive adjective "whose" is needed to show ownership of the purse.

---

**Q16.** The _______ of the forest was broken only by the _______ chirping of birds.
- (a) beauty, loud
- (b) silence, occasional
- (c) density, constant
- (d) greenery, melodious

> **Answer: (b)** — "broken" logically connects with "silence" being interrupted.

---

**Q17.** He _______ hard for the exam and _______ with flying colors.
- (a) studies, passes
- (b) studied, passed
- (c) has studied, passed
- (d) was studying, had passed

> **Answer: (b)** — Both actions are in simple past (completed actions).

## 7. Para Jumbles

**Q37.** Arrange the following sentences in the correct order:

P. However, the technology is still in its early stages and faces numerous challenges.
Q. Self-driving cars have been a dream of automotive engineers for decades.
R. Despite these challenges, major companies continue to invest billions in the technology.
S. These include regulatory hurdles, safety concerns, and the complexity of real-world driving conditions.
T. They believe that autonomous vehicles will revolutionize transportation within the next decade.

- (a) QPSRT  (b) QRPST  (c) PQRST  (d) QPRST

> **Answer: (a) QPSRT**
> Q (intro) → P (however, contrast) → S (these = challenges) → R (despite) → T (they = companies)

---

**Q38.** Rearrange:

A. But in the modern era, its significance has grown multifold.
B. Education has always been valued in every civilization.
C. This is because a knowledge-based economy demands highly skilled individuals.
D. In ancient times, it was limited to a privileged few.
E. Today, access to education is considered a fundamental right.

- (a) BDAEC  (b) BDEAC  (c) BDAEC  (d) BDEAC

> **Answer: (a) BDAEC**
> B (general) → D (ancient times) → A (but modern era) → E (today) → C (because, reason)

## 8. Cloze Test

**Q39–43.** Fill in the blanks:

> The importance of education in today's world cannot be (39)______. Education not only (40)______ individuals with knowledge but also (41)______ them to think critically. In a rapidly (42)______ world, those who fail to (43)______ risk being left behind.

**Q39.** (a) denied  (b) overstated  (c) ignored  (d) accepted
> **Answer: (b) overstated** — "cannot be overstated" = is very important

**Q40.** (a) equips  (b) fills  (c) loads  (d) charges
> **Answer: (a) equips** — "equips with knowledge" is correct collocation

**Q41.** (a) forces  (b) enables  (c) stops  (d) prevents
> **Answer: (b) enables** — "enables them to think"

**Q42.** (a) moving  (b) slowing  (c) changing  (d) stopping
> **Answer: (c) changing** — "rapidly changing world"

**Q43.** (a) adapt  (b) sleep  (c) eat  (d) withdraw
> **Answer: (a) adapt** — "fail to adapt" fits the context

## 9. Sentence Improvement

**Q44.** "He **said me** to complete the assignment by Friday."
- (a) told me
- (b) said to me
- (c) asked me
- (d) No improvement

> **Answer: (a) told me** — "told" takes a direct object; "said" requires "to"

---

**Q45.** "In spite of the fact that he was tired, he continued working."
Better way to write this:
- (a) Although he was tired, he continued working.
- (b) Despite the fact he was tired, he continued.
- (c) Because he was tired, he continued.
- (d) No improvement

> **Answer: (a)** — More concise and natural phrasing.

---

**Q46.** "He is **more taller** than his brother."
- (a) more tall
- (b) taller
- (c) most taller
- (d) No improvement

> **Answer: (b) taller** — Never use "more" with comparative adjectives ending in -er.

---

**Q47.** "He **prevented me to go** to the party."
- (a) prevented me from going
- (b) prevented me going
- (c) prevented me for going
- (d) No improvement

> **Answer: (a) prevented me from going** — Correct idiom: "prevent from + gerund"

## 10. Active & Passive Voice

**Q48.** Change to passive: "The company launched a new product."
- (a) A new product was launched by the company.
- (b) A new product is launched by the company.
- (c) A new product has been launched by the company.
- (d) A new product had been launched by the company.

> **Answer: (a)** — Simple Past Active → Simple Past Passive (was + V3)

---

**Q49.** Change to active: "The novel was written by Charles Dickens."
- (a) Charles Dickens wrote the novel.
- (b) Charles Dickens writes the novel.
- (c) Charles Dickens has written the novel.
- (d) The novel has been written.

> **Answer: (a)** — Past passive → Simple past active

---

**Q50.** "Someone stole my wallet." (Convert to passive)
- (a) My wallet was stolen by someone.
- (b) My wallet was stolen.
- (c) My wallet has been stolen.
- (d) Both (a) and (b) are correct

> **Answer: (d)** — Both are valid, though (b) is preferred (dropping "by someone").

## 11. Direct & Indirect Speech

**Q51.** Change to indirect: He said, "I am going to school."
- (a) He said that he was going to school.
- (b) He said that he is going to school.
- (c) He told that he was going to school.
- (d) He said he has been going to school.

> **Answer: (a)** — Present continuous → Past continuous. "said" + "that" is correct.

---

**Q52.** Change to indirect: She said, "I will come tomorrow."
- (a) She said that she will come tomorrow.
- (b) She said that she would come the next day.
- (c) She told that she would come tomorrow.
- (d) She said she comes the next day.

> **Answer: (b)** — "will" → "would", "tomorrow" → "the next day"

---

**Q53.** Change to indirect: He said to me, "Do you like coffee?"
- (a) He asked me if I liked coffee.
- (b) He asked me that I like coffee.
- (c) He asked me do I like coffee.
- (d) He told me if I liked coffee.

> **Answer: (a)** — Yes/No questions → "asked + if/whether"

## 12. Articles & Prepositions

**Q54.** Choose the correct article: "He is _____ honest man."
- (a) a  (b) an  (c) the  (d) no article

> **Answer: (b) an** — Silent 'h' → "an honest man"

---

**Q55.** "She is _____ university student."
- (a) a  (b) an  (c) the  (d) no article

---

**Q56.** Choose the correct preposition: "He is good _____ mathematics."

> **Answer: (b) at** — "good at" is the correct collocation

---

**Q57.** "She insisted _____ going to the party."

## 13. Subject-Verb Agreement

**Q58.** "A number of students _____ present in the class."
- (a) is  (b) are  (c) was  (d) has been

> **Answer: (b) are** — "A number of" takes plural verb.

---

**Q59.** "The number of applicants _____ increasing every year."
- (a) are  (b) is  (c) were  (d) have been

> **Answer: (b) is** — "The number of" takes singular verb.

---

**Q60.** "Bread and butter _____ his daily breakfast."
- (a) are  (b) is  (c) were  (d) have been

> **Answer: (b) is** — "Bread and butter" refers to one item → singular.

## 14. Tenses

**Q61.** "By the time he arrives, we _____ the work."
- (a) finish  (b) will finish  (c) will have finished  (d) had finished

> **Answer: (c) will have finished** — Future perfect for action completed before another future action.

---

**Q62.** "I _____ him for five years." (I still know him)
- (a) know  (b) knew  (c) have known  (d) had known

> **Answer: (c) have known** — Present perfect for ongoing action started in the past.

---

**Q63.** "She _____ to Paris three times so far."
- (a) went  (b) goes  (c) has gone  (d) had gone

> **Answer: (c) has gone** — "so far" → present perfect

## 🎯 Quick Revision — Common Verbal Ability Traps

| Trap | Rule |
|------|------|
| "Each/Every" | Always singular verb |
| "Neither...nor" | Verb agrees with nearest subject |
| "Since/For" | Use present perfect (continuous) |
| "Said me" | ❌ Wrong → Use "told me" or "said to me" |
| "More taller" | ❌ Never double comparative |
| "An university" | ❌ Wrong → "A university" (consonant sound /j/) |
| "An honest" | ✅ Correct (silent h) |
| "Prevented to go" | ❌ → "Prevented from going" |
| "A number of" | Plural verb |
| "The number of" | Singular verb |
